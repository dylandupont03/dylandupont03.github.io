Could Brice Williams have been an All-American?

bricewilliams
all-american
collegebasketball
Author

Dylan DuPont

Published

April 14, 2025

5th-year guard Brice Williams had a phenomenal senior season for the Nebraska Cornhuskers breaking the single season scoring record previously held by guard James Palmer Jr. Williams averaged 20.4 points, 4.1 rebounds, 2.9 assists, and 1.1 steals on 47.1 FG%, 37 three point%, and 88.3% from the line.

Although the Huskers came up just short of making the NCAA Tournament, could Brice Williams have made an All-American team had they finished with a better record. Let’s dive into some deeper analytics.

There is an advanced metric called z-scores which is the number of standard deviations by which the value of a raw is above or below the mean value. Williams is 10 stand deviations higher than the mean value when looking across the entire NCAA, that only places him ahead of 2 All-Americans, Houston’s L.J. Cryer and Texas Tech’s JT Toppin. The All-American with the highest z-score was Purdue’s Braden Smith with a staggering 18 z-score, makes sense that he won the Bob Cousy Award (best point guard in the NCAA).

cbbplayers <- load_mbb_player_box(2025)

totals <- cbbplayers |> 
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_short_display_name, ) |> 
  summarise(
    totalmins = sum(minutes, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalpts = sum(points, na.rm=TRUE), 
    totaloffrebs = sum(offensive_rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    totaldefrebs = sum(defensive_rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalrebs = sum(rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalasts = sum(assists, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalstls = sum(steals, na.rm=TRUE), 
    totalblks = sum(blocks, na.rm=TRUE),
    totaltos = sum(turnovers, na.rm=TRUE), 
    totalfouls = sum(fouls, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalfgsmade = sum(field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalfgsatt = sum(field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE),
    total3ptsmade = sum(three_point_field_goals_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    total3ptsatt = sum(three_point_field_goals_attempted, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalftsmade = sum(free_throws_made, na.rm=TRUE),
    totalftsatt = sum(free_throws_attempted, na.rm=TRUE)
  ) |> 
  ungroup()

total_zscores <- totals |>
  filter(totalmins > 50) |> 
  mutate(
    ptsZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalpts, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    rbZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalrebs, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    mpZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalmins, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    astZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalasts, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    ftsmadeZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalftsmade, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    stlZscore = as.numeric(scale(totalstls, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    Score = ptsZscore + rbZscore + mpZscore + astZscore + ftsmadeZscore + stlZscore
  ) |> 
  arrange(desc(Score))

Scoring wise, Brice Williams actually averaged more points per game than 13 players across the All-American teams. He is up there amongst the best in scoring, as he finished 13th in the country at 20.4 PPG. The only 2 All-Americans he finished below were Villanova’s Eric Dixon (23.3) and Memphis’ PJ Haggerty (21.7). Williams being above 13 of the 2024-25 All-Americans show’s why he was elite at putting the ball in the basket this season. He even had a better scoring season than the NBA’s projected #1 pick in the 2025 Draft, Duke’s Cooper Flagg.

One of the most important advanced analytical stats is how valuable a player is to their team when they are on the court. Via EvanMiya, The BPR is the Bayesian Performance Rating is the sum of their offensive and defensive BPR that measures a player’s overall value to his team when they are on the court.

And the Box BPR is a sum of a player’s Box offensive and defensive BPR, it’s an estimate of a player’s overall value based only on this player’s individuals box score stats. You want a higher rating in both advanced statistics (BPR and BoxBPR).

All in all looking deeper into analytics, even had Nebraska finished with a better record. It’s very unlikely that Brice Williams’ 2024-25 campaign would have resulted in an All-American nod, especially with there being way bigger names and the Huskers not being a household name in the college basketball world.
