Who Should Win the 2024-25 NBA MVP?

The 2024-25 NBA season has had a wild Most Valuable Player (MVP) race that has been up and down all year long. The top 5 MVP candidates on a lot of people’s have been Denver’s Nikola Jokic, Oklahoma City’s Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, Boston’s Jayson Tatum, Milwaukee’s Giannis Antetokounmpo, and New York’s Jalen Brunson. Most people expect the race to come down to either Jokic or SGA.

Gilgeous-Alexander had a fantastic season as he won the NBA scoring title (most PPG in the league) with 32.7 points per game, which is 2.3 higher than the next player Giannis (30.4).

Although Jokic didn’t rack up as many points as Shai, the Joker dominated the entire season in every aspect. He averaged a triple-double, which is the 7th time in NBA history someone has averaged a triple-double across an entire NBA season. Jokic is the 3rd player in NBA history to average a triple-double over a season and the first big man to ever do it. Oscar Robertson did it once and Jokic’s current teammate, Russell Westbrook, did it 4 times.

library(tidyverse)
library(hoopR)
library(gt)
library(ggalt)
library(ggtext)

nbaplayers <- load_nba_player_box(2025)

players <- nbaplayers |> 
  filter(season_type != "5" & season_type != "3" & team_name != "Team Kenny" & team_name != "Team Shaq" & team_name != "Team Candace" & team_name != "Team Chuck")

avgs <- players |> 
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_display_name) |> 
  summarise(
    mpg = mean(minutes, na.rm=TRUE),
    ppg = mean(points, na.rm=TRUE),
    rpg = mean(rebounds, na.rm=TRUE),
    apg = mean(assists, na.rm=TRUE),
    stl = mean(steals, na.rm=TRUE),
  ) |> 
  ungroup()

mvpavgs <- avgs |>

One of the most important things on the court is obviously helping your team gain points vs turning the ball the over and getting no points. The good thing about the top 5 candidates is that they are all good at sharing the ball. All 5 players averaged 6.0 assists per game or more.

Jokic absolutely dominated the NBA in passing as he posted 716 assists and only 230 turnovers, something of a center that was unheard of until Nikola Jokic graced the NBA. His 716 assists broke his own record (708) for most assists in a season by a center. Next season, the Joker will already break Kareem Abdul-Jabbar’s record for most all-time regular season assists (5,660). He will only need 278 assists to capture another assist record.

aststotosratio <- players |> 
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_display_name) |> 
  summarise(
    asts = sum(assists, na.rm=TRUE),
    tos = sum(turnovers, na.rm=TRUE),
  ) |> 
  ungroup()

top10 <- c("Shai Gilgeous-Alexander", "Nikola Jokic", "Jayson Tatum", "Giannis Antetokounmpo", "Jalen Brunson", "Cade Cunningham", "Stephen Curry", "Donovan Mitchell", "LeBron James", "Anthony Edwards")

top10asttoturn <- aststotosratio |> 
  filter(athlete_display_name %in% top10) |> 
  rename(Team = team_display_name) |> 
  rename(Player = athlete_display_name)

ggplot() +

When looking at most people’s top 5 candidates for MVP, it’s clear that the top 2 most deserving are Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and Nikola Jokic, they run away from the rest. Jokic’s insane season of averaging a triple-double again puts him ahead of SGA, although you can’t discredit SGA’s incredible scoring season. Jokic could very well be onto his 4th MVP and 2nd consecutive.
